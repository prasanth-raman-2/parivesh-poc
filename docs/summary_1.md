## 1) Document overview (project, location, proponents, regulatory context)

### Project proponent & facility
- **Company:** PCBL (TN) Limited (carbon black manufacturing with captive power generation)
- **Existing facility:** Carbon black plant in **SIPCOT Industrial Park, Thervoy Kandigai Village**, Gummidipoondi Taluk, Tiruvallur District, Tamil Nadu.

### Proposed change / purpose
- **Expansion of carbon black capacity:** **550 TPD → 675 TPD** (annualized: **192,500 TPA → 236,250 TPA**) via an additional production line within/adjacent to the existing site.
- **Captive power:** **36 MW Waste Heat Recovery Boiler (WHRB)-based cogeneration captive power plant** using off-gas from the carbon black process.

### Land and setting
- **Total land:** **62.46 acres** (≈ **25.2767 ha**), including **additional 2.44 acres** (adjacent) for expansion.
- Located inside an approved **industrial estate (SIPCOT)**; land-use certificate indicates industrial zoning.

### Regulatory category and appraisal basis
- Activity referenced as **Item 5(e)** (petrochemical-based processing) under EIA Notification 2006.
- Though normally Category B, the report states appraisal as **Category A** due to **Tamil Nadu–Andhra Pradesh interstate boundary within 5 km** (about **2.67 km** reported).
- **ToR issued by MoEF&CC:** File no. **IA-J-11011/95/2024-IA-II(I)** dated **14.03.2024**.
- **Public hearing:** **Exempted**, because SIPCOT Industrial Complex had prior EC (SIPCOT EC referenced: MoEF&CC letter No. 21-41/2009-IA III dated 09/08/2010).

### EIA consultant
- **ABC Techno Labs India Pvt. Ltd.** (NABET accredited; NABL/MoEF&CC recognition details included in report).

## 2) Executive summary — key outcomes

- Expansion is proposed in an already industrialized SIPCOT area with existing infrastructure.
- Baseline environmental monitoring was conducted **March–May 2021** and later **revalidated in Feb 2024** for key parameters.
- **Air dispersion modelling (AERMOD)** indicates incremental concentrations are small and **resultant concentrations remain within standards**.
- Water management emphasizes **recycle/reuse with RO + MEE** leading to **Zero Liquid Discharge (ZLD)**.
- Noise modelling and mitigation (acoustic enclosures, PPE, greenbelt) are proposed to keep impacts within permissible limits.
- Risk assessment indicates **no offsite population impacts** for modeled scenarios; LSIR/F–N curves not significant for offsite reach.

## 3) Project description (existing vs proposed; process; utilities; resources; wastes)

### Products & capacity
- **Different grades of carbon black:** existing 550 TPD; proposed additional 125 TPD → total **675 TPD**.
- **CPP:** **36 MW** (off-gas/waste heat based cogeneration).

### Process summary (carbon black & energy integration)
- Carbon black is produced by **thermal cracking** of highly aromatic hydrocarbon oils in a **specially designed reactor**.
- Carbon black particles are separated from product gases using **Process Bag Collectors (pulse-jet filters)**; recovered carbon black is **pelletized and dried**.
- **Off-gas** (low-Btu) is collected in an off-gas header and **fully utilized** as fuel in:
  - **Waste heat boiler** (high pressure steam generation for power), and
  - **Dryer combustor furnace** (pellet drying heat).
- Key pollution-control/process integration units described include:
  - **Process Bag Collectors (PBC)**
  - **Vapor Bag Collectors (VBC)**
  - **Venturi scrubbers** (emergency shutdown mitigation)
  - **Flare stack** and **start-up vent stack**

### Utilities (water & power)
- **Water requirement after expansion:** **3953 KLD** total.
  - **Fresh water:** **3737 KLD** from **SIPCOT**.
  - **Recycled/recirculated:** about **683 KLD** (treated and reused).
  - **Domestic water:** reported ~**57 KLD**; sewage generation around **48 KLD**.
- **Power:**
  - Existing requirement ~**10 MW**; additional ~**1.8 MW** → total ~**11.8 MW**.
  - **36 MW CPP** provides power; excess can be exported.

### Wastewater management (ZLD)
- Industrial effluent treated in **MBR-based ETP (210 KLD)** → filtration (PSF/ACF) → **UF** → **two-stage RO**.
- **RO permeate reused** in process.
- **RO reject to MEE** to achieve **ZLD**.
- Sewage treated in **MBR-based STP (50 KLD)** and reused for **greenbelt/plantation**.
- **Specific water consumption** improves from **5.06 → 4.67 KLD/tonne** (reported).

### Air emissions and control (summary)
- Main expected emissions are **SOx and NOx** from combustion; PM from process controlled by bag filters.
- Stacks include boiler stacks (~**90 m**) and dryer stacks (~**60 m**) (as described in air modelling/control sections).
- **Online CEMS** in process stacks with connectivity to TNPCB server; compliance with applicable petrochemical emission norms referenced.

### Solid & hazardous waste (high-level)
- **Municipal solid waste:** ~**260 kg/day** after expansion; segregation and disposal via local panchayat per SWM Rules 2016.
- **Hazardous wastes:** stored in designated areas with bunding; recyclables to authorized recyclers; residual disposal via TSDF.

### Greenbelt & land use
- Greenbelt within premises reported as **83,589 m² (~33% of plot area)**.
- Native species listed (e.g., neem, pungai, teak, mahogany, etc.).

### Employment
- Existing employment around **300**; expansion adds **~60** jobs (mix of permanent/contract).
- Construction-phase manpower peak around **500**.

### Project cost
- **Total project cost:** **₹360 crore**.

## 5) Anticipated impacts & mitigation measures

### Construction phase
- Site already levelled/paved; major site clearing and road construction largely not required.
- **Stormwater/runoff:** estimated annual runoff increase from **140,690 m³/yr → 305,366 m³/yr** post-construction (increase ~**164,676 m³/yr**). Runoff directed to **rainwater harvesting pond (RHP)** and overflow to canal.
- Erosion/dust/stormwater controls: scheduling excavation in non-monsoon, material storage controls, water sprinkling, wind curtains.
- Construction noise modelling indicates attenuation with distance; nearest village impacts limited.

### Operation phase — air environment
- Emission estimation based on assumed outlet concentrations and stack parameters; contributions from point sources and traffic included.
- **AERMOD modelling (incremental + baseline):**
  - PM resultant at project site ~**78.28 µg/m³** vs standard **150 µg/m³** (as presented)
  - NOx and SOx resultant values presented as within standards.
- Mitigation includes closed-loop transfer, pneumatic conveying with bag filters, adequate stack heights, online monitoring, greenbelt.

### Noise
- Major sources: compressors, cooling towers, blowers, pumps, reactors, boilers, turbines, vehicles.
- Noise propagation modelling indicates max around **59.6 dB(A) at 1.5 km** (without attenuation) and reduced further with mitigation.
- Controls: acoustic enclosures, PPE (ear plugs/muffs), maintenance, rotation, greenbelt.

### Water & wastewater
- No discharge to surface/groundwater: **ETP + UF/RO + MEE** and **STP** reuse.
- Additional controls: stormwater drainage, RWH, segregation of effluent from stormwater.

### Land/soil & waste
- MSW handled per SWM Rules 2016; hazardous waste per applicable hazardous waste rules; bunding and secure storage.

### Biodiversity
- Primary impact pathway is through air/noise; mitigation via emissions control and greenbelt.
- Conservation plan prepared for **Schedule-I fauna in buffer** (e.g., Indian peafowl, raptors, flapshell turtle) and to be vetted with Forest Department.

### Traffic
- Expansion traffic increase negligible; LOS remains A.

### Occupational health & safety
- Hazards: flammable storages (CBFS, SFS, diesel), noise, heat, dust, chemicals.
- OHS provisions: OHC, PPE matrix, periodic medical exams, emergency response teams, firefighting systems, mock drills.

## 12) Conclusions and commitments (report conclusion)

- Expansion impacts are assessed as **localized, short-term, and low-to-moderate**, mainly during construction.
